2. Screen Capture

Zenkai captures your screen during AI training sessions.

What This Means

When you start a training session in Zenkai Dojo, the software captures frames from your screen at high frequency (typically 10–30 frames per second). This is how the AI agent "sees" the game.

What Gets Captured

  • The full screen or specific display area where the game is running
  • Any other application windows, notifications, or desktop content visible at the time
  • This may include sensitive information such as messages, emails, passwords, financial data, or personal content that happens to be visible

How Captures Are Processed

  • Screen frames are processed locally on your machine in real-time
  • Frames are fed directly to the AI model for inference (deciding what action to take)
  • Frames are not saved as image files — they exist in memory only during processing
  • Frames are never transmitted to Zenkai Corporation servers or any third party
  • Training metrics (reward scores, episode counts, accuracy) are logged locally as numerical data, not images

Your Responsibility

Before starting any training session, you should:

  • Close all sensitive applications (banking, messaging, email, documents with personal data)
  • Disable desktop notifications (including messaging apps, email alerts, system notifications)
  • Ensure only the target game is visible on your primary display
  • Be aware that any content visible on your screen during training will be processed by the AI

3. Mouse & Keyboard Control

Zenkai controls your mouse and keyboard during AI training sessions.

What This Means

When a training session is active, Zenkai takes control of your mouse cursor and sends keystrokes to allow the AI agent to interact with the game. During this time:

  • Your mouse cursor will be moved by the AI — you will not be able to use it normally
  • The AI will click, move, and scroll as part of gameplay
  • The AI may send keystrokes to the active window
  • Your computer is effectively unusable for other tasks during training

Risks

  • Bugs in the software could cause unintended mouse clicks or keystrokes outside the game window
  • If another application gains focus during training, the AI may interact with it unintentionally
  • Fast, repeated mouse movements and clicks may trigger anti-cheat systems in some games
  • Input control errors could potentially modify, delete, or send content in other applications

Safety Controls

  • Emergency stop: Press Ctrl+Shift+Q at any time to immediately halt all AI input
  • Stop button: The training interface includes a Stop button (though you may need the hotkey if your mouse is being controlled)
  • Auto-checkpoint: Training progress is saved automatically at regular intervals

4. Hardware Risks

AI training is computationally intensive. During training sessions:

  • CPU and GPU usage will be elevated, potentially to 100%
  • System temperatures will increase
  • Fan noise will increase
  • Extended high-load operation can accelerate hardware wear
  • Systems with inadequate cooling may experience thermal throttling or, in extreme cases, hardware damage

Zenkai includes hardware monitoring and safety warnings. We strongly recommend using these features and not overriding temperature warnings.
