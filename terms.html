4. Screen Capture and Input Control

Important: Zenkai captures your screen and controls your mouse and keyboard during AI training sessions. This section explains exactly what that means.

What Zenkai Does During Training

  • Screen capture: Zenkai reads pixels from your screen to allow the AI agent to "see" the game. This captures whatever is visible on your display during training.
  • Mouse control: Zenkai moves your mouse cursor and clicks to allow the AI agent to play the game.
  • Keyboard control: Zenkai sends keystrokes to control game actions.

When It Happens

  • Only when you explicitly start a training session
  • Never in the background or without your knowledge
  • You can stop at any time using the Stop button or Ctrl+Shift+Q

What You Should Know

  • Screen capture may inadvertently capture sensitive information visible on your screen (notifications, messages, other applications)
  • Close sensitive applications and disable notifications before starting training
  • During training, your computer is not usable for other tasks — the AI is controlling mouse and keyboard
  • Bugs in the software could cause unintended mouse clicks or keystrokes
  • Screen capture data is processed locally and is not transmitted to our servers

Your Consent

Screen capture and input control require your explicit consent each time you start a training session. You may withdraw consent at any time by stopping the training session. See our Privacy Policy for details on how screen capture data is processed.
